How to Take Down Notes More Effectively



If your in-class notes are messy, not organized, and unclear from the start, you're not going to get a lot of use out of them. This has nothing to do with how neat your handwriting is â€” it's about how your notes are structured.

Perhaps the best way to remember (and comprehend) what you are learning in class is to take effective notes in the classroom.

WHY ARE EFFECTIVE NOTE TAKING SKILLS IMPORTANT?

Better notes will help you with remember concepts, create meaningful learning skills, and gain a better understanding of a topic. Effective notes will even prompt less stress when test time arrives!

Figuring out how to take better study notes in class improves recall and understanding of what you are learning on because it:

  • Guarantees you are actively tuning in to what the teacher is saying
  • Requires you to think what you are writing
  • Helps you with making connection between topics
  • Serves as quality review material for after class

Using different note taking methodologies is important, particularly as you progress through high school and go to college or university. There are a few note taking techniques you can use to begin taking better notes in class.


TRY THESE 5 METHODS TO FIND THE BEST NOTE TAKING METHOD FOR YOU!

THE CORNELL METHOD

The Cornell note taking method puts together class notes into easily digestible summaries. This method is effective in light of the fact that the main point, details, study cues, and summary are totally written in one place.

ADVANTAGES:

  • Notes are nicely organized, summed up, and easy to review
  • Permits you to pull out major concepts and ideas

WHAT DOES IT LOOK LIKE?

The paper is divided into 3 sections: a 2.5" margin to the left side, a 2" summary section on the bottom, and a main 6" in-class note section.

  • Utilize the main notes section to take notes during class.
  • Use the cues section to review your notes. After class, write down things you'll have to remember and a brief for each. You can likewise utilize this part of the section for vocabulary words and study questions.
  • In the summary segment at the bottom, write a summary of your notes. This is the place where you will highlight the main points.


THE MAPPING METHOD

The Mapping note taking method is a more visual approach to arrange your class notes. This method is helpful when learning about relationships between topics.

ADVANTAGES:

  • Useful for visual learner students who struggle with studying from notes.
  • Helps you with remember and associate relationships between topics.

WHAT DOES IT LOOK LIKE?

The page is organized by topic. The main topic branch out into subtopics with detailed information about each.

HOW DO YOU USE IT?

  • While in class, start the map with the main topic.
  • Branching off the main subject, write a heading for each of the subtopics.
  • Write any important notes under each subtopic.
  • Proceed with the pattern.


THE OUTLINING METHOD

The Outlining note taking method utilizes headings and bullet to sort out topics. This method is most helpful when learning about topics that include a lot of detail.

ADVANTAGES:

  • Permits notes to be neatly organized.
  • It is not difficult to see the relationship among topics and subtopics.
  • Easy to turn points into study questions.

WHAT DOES IT LOOK LIKE?

Each segment begins with a heading of the main topic. Each subtopic and supporting facts is written underneath the proper heading.

HOW DO YOU USE IT?

  • During a lesson, start your notes with a single bullet point and write the main topic.
  • Place the first subtopic underneath and indented slightly to the right.
  • List any details beneath your heading and slightly to the right.


THE CHARTING METHOD

Charting note taking method utilizes columns to organize information. This method is valuable for lessons that cover a ton of facts or relationships between topics.

ADVANTAGES:

  • Facts are organized and easy to review.
  • Highlights key pieces of information for every topic.

WHAT DOES IT LOOK LIKE?

The page is separated into columns labeled by category. The details of each category are rounded out in the lines beneath.

HOW DO YOU USE IT?

  • When information about a category is mentioned, write it down under the appropriate column.
  • When the next topic starts to move down one row, begin again.


THE SENTENCE METHOD

The Sentence note taking method is simply writing every topic as a scribble note sentence. This technique works well for fast paced lessons where tons of information is being covered.

ADVANTAGES:

  • Writing down main points helps you with figuring out which information is important and which isn't.
  • You can cover a lot of details and information rapidly.
  • Notes are simplified for study and review.

WHAT DOES IT LOOK LIKE?

Each line on the page is new and separate topic. To arrange your notes even more, you can utilize headings for every main topic.

HOW DO YOU USE IT?

  • Write important information the teacher has emphasized. This can be in sentence structure or point structure.
  • Start another sentence or point for each new detail.
  • Use headings to organize points by main points.


BETTER GRADES START WITH BETTER NOTES

Taking organized and smart notes can help improve your understanding and recall of what you have learned in class. try these methods for note taking in your next classes and see which one turns out best for you!

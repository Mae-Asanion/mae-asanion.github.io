Student Testimonial



As a student, I made use of a lot of resources to get me through difficult times. I have tried a lot of things to make me a better student, and I still am. Sometimes it doesn’t stick, and truth be told, it’s somewhat rare if it does. Humans are creatures of habit and similarly, students’ habits are what makes or breaks them. Some might say that school is not for everybody if that’s the case, and they’d be right. Some are more predisposed to physical learning than visual, some children have mental disabilities and handicaps that might never allow them to excel in the way others do because the educational system mostly caters to a select few.

The best the rest of us can do is try to catch up. Whether this means keeping a planner or a bullet journal, I’ve been trying for a long time. I look at pinterest boards and study with me videos on youtube of people with picture perfect notes and grades I never got on all subjects. I sit, enamoured by the world they’ve presented to me. They provide tips for how they achieved their goals and advice on what to do. Their words flow like water into my hands, and they flow out gaps of my fingers as I try to drink them in.

I don’t think I’ll ever be at the level that they have achieved academically, and I’ve accepted that. What worked for them won’t alway work on me. But this is a journey all of us have to keep taking for most of our lives. Learning doesn’t just end when you’re handed a diploma. I will continue to try my hardest to learn.



Article written by Jana Rinoah Polonan

A student from University of the East. Date modified: May 7, 2021

How to budget your money as a Student



  • Set Financial Goals - If you want to save money, you should identify how much you can reasonably round up according to your allowance or income, whichever one applies to you. This could involve determining how much money you gain in a month and checking how much you spend. That way you can see what you can cut back on.
  • Track your expenses - Make a record of all the expenses you know of. Even the little things like you buying a piece of gum to freshen your breath. Those little things could amount to a bigger mass.
  • Plan for all types of expenses - You should plan for all types of expenses. Sometimes, your parents don’t shoulder the cost for your leisure. This could involve things like netflix and spotify subscriptions. Plan out your payment for these kinds of expenses in advance.
  • Plan for infrequent expenses - You should allot a bit of petty cash in your budget to be able to account for things that don’t annually need to be spent on. This could include things like a new pair of shoes, in case yours broke, or a gift for a friend’s birthday.
  • Use accurate descriptions - Always know what you’re spending money on. In your notes, try to explain as much as you can why the expense was needed, that way you don’t forget.
  • Update your budget daily - Try to take the time to sit down at the end of the day to update your budget plan. Try to recall all the expenses you had for the day and update your record accordingly.

How to ace writing a narrative essay



  • Consider the topic - always consider what the topic of the story is about. This provides context to your readers, and will help you keep the nature of your paper in mind.
  • Start with the draft - Just begin writing. It doesn’t have to be perfect or follow certain guidelines. Just start by writing the summary of the story and what you have gleaned from the text.
  • Storyline elements - Ascertain the elements of the story. This could refer to anything from, characters, setting and plot.
  • The point of view - The point of view must always be acknowledged as it is the way in which you can see the world or events that take place in the story.
  • Proving and supporting - Provide your own insight upon reading the story and show some concepts that support it.
  • Do not give details that don’t mean anything to your story - Do not confuse your readers by putting redundant information in your paper.
  • Clear writing - In a similar vein, do not use overly complicated vocabulary terms. Use clear and precise language to fully describe your point and message in the most accurate way possible.

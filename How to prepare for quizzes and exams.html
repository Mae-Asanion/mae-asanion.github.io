How to Prepare for Quizzes and Exams



Quiz and Exam Tips

The following are proven procedures that have helped numerous students with improving grades and plan all the more adequately for quizzes and exams

Don't Procrastinate. Don't Cram.

It appears as though some thrive on last-minute 'cramming'. Yet, most experts will concur, cramming isn't effective. The most concerning issue with cramming, as indicated by UCLA educator of psychiatry Andrew Fuligni, is that cramming makes students sacrifice sleep time in order to study. While it's unrealistic to perform well on a test without the necessary knowledge, sleep is critical for scholastic achievement. Sacrificing sleep for an extra 3 or 4 hours of study is frequentlycounterproductive.

Quite possibly the most important test preparation tips we can give you is to give yourself sufficient time to study. Try not to procrastinate! It's ideal to start getting ready well before the day of a test. Make it a habit to complete reading assignments on time and frequently review lecture notes. In case you're getting ready for midterm or final exams,you'll need to put organize your time so you can dedicate more time to those exams that are more important or will be really demanding.

Plan Your Study Time

Make time for studying. Setting aside regular time to read and study is critical for achieving high exam/quiz results. We suggest setting up a term calendar, week after week timetable and every day plan that includes regular study sessions. It's so easy for work and other activities to quickly take precedence over studying, so plan your study time ahead of time. As you progress through the term it's OK to change your study timetable to address your needs, yet ensure you plan study sessions ahead of time – and that you stick with them.

Watch for Clues

Almost every teacher has a methodology in making test questions – and they will in general utilize a similar methodology each time they prepare a test. Watch for clues your instructor may give about possible test questions and formats. It is advisable to partake at test review sessions and ask questions about unclear ideas. Never be ashamed to ask questions.

Ask Your Teacher for Direction

We'd even suggest asking your teacher directly the most ideal approach to study for the test. Many teachers are open to sharing with students suggestions, even secrets, with regards to how best plan for their exams. Try not to be frightened to get some information about what subjects or topics you will be tested on during the exam – even what sorts of questions tomight see.

Arrive Early on Test Day

The more you can gather before a test the better you'll be. I actually recall showing up for class early on the day of my AP History exam in high school. My teacher, Mr. Farr, was responding to a question asked to him by another student looking for some explanation on test subject matter. Regardless of whether attentively or accidentally I'll never know however he said to this student, "Don't stress about that. Very little of the test covers that material." Knowing that permitted me to focus my remaining 30 minutes of study on subject that would have been bound to be on the test.

It's also common for students struggling with difficult concepts or subject matter to show up early on test day to seek additional clarification or understanding from the teacher or instructor. This is an excellent opportunity to gain additional understanding and test preparation.

Make sure to show up to class early on exam day, in case the teacher provides any additional tips, instruction or insight.

Review Early. Review With a Group.

Start a final review of all lecture notes, reading assignments, and other class materials that will help you be prepared, several days prior to the test. You can do this review by yourself or as a part of a study group. Working as part of a study group is an excellent method to review in preparation for a test. Reviewing in preparation for a test as part of a study group will allow you to improve your notes, fill in any holes in your understanding, more fully explore complex concepts, maximize your time, cover more material, gain additional knowledge about what might be on the test, and provide you with a support system.

Prepare an Outline

Prepare an outline of the main topics and concepts that will be covered during a test, at that point utilize this sheet to study. This will assist you with memorizing key facts and other information you will be tested on.

Use Visual Aids

Visual aids, including charts, diagrams and graphs are great study helps – especially if you're a visual learner. Organizing information into diagrams and charts helps to condense and simplify information and improves recall at test time.

Stay Healthy

Getting plenty of rest and exercising regularly will upgrade your capacity to perform well on a test. Eat a nutritious foods before taking an exam to be ready and focused. Avoid junk food. Eating lousy junk food or sugar before a test will cause your energy levels to drain rapidly and can affect your ability to focus.

Get Plenty of Sleep

It is unwise to remain up throughout the late evening studying before test day. Get plenty of sleep the night prior to a test. Although spending extra time preparing seems like it will improve your score, you never want to show up to a test tired. Studies show that students who spend more time studying and less time cramming the prior night normally perform better on tests.

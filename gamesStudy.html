Can games help me study?



Although it’s a bit of a cliche, if you ask a parent why they think their child is failing their classes, most of them will say video games almost unanimously. While it is true that an excess of the time spent playing video is far from productive, what do you think about a controlled interval where people are allowed to play to their hearts’ content? Or a game that has some educational value attached to it?

Research done by the University of Arkansas states that even just an hour of playing moba games like Valorant, or League of Legends stimulates people’s brains by increasing performance on tests of visual selective activity. Stating that short intervals of playing can change brain activity and improve attention skills.

Similarly, some games possess educational value. There are even a few games solely dedicated to that. When it comes to children’s leisurely activities maybe the answer is not to take away what they find enjoyable, but instead limit their indulgences so they don’t become vices.

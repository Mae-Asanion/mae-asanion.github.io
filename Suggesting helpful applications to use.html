Helpful Applications for Students



There's (almost) an application for everything these days, and this can make student life easier, less expensive, more secure and more fun. Regardless of whether you need assistance with taking notes, revising, waking up on time or staying in shape, read on for our pick of the applications for students.

1. Lecture capture apps

Just a couple of years ago, students attending to lectures would need to spend their whole energy writing wildly on notepads, in order not to miss any vital piece of information. At that point along came technology, and now, lecture capture applications are a reality. Although the recording of lectures has been common practice within universities for some time, lecture capture applications permit you to record and tune in back to classes without spending additional money on costly recording equipment.

SoundNote (iOS) is a popular lecture capture application for iPad users, going about as a notepad and audio recorder, so you can store a whole lecture in both visual and sound form. In the event that it's a whiteboard you need to capture, in any case, Office Lens permits users to photograph a whiteboard, convert it to a PDF, Word or PowerPoint file and store all the data via OneNote or OneDrive for catch-up and revision purposes.

Other than SoundNote, other good quality lecture capture apps include Notes Plus (iOS) and Audio Memos Free â€“ The Voice Recorder (iOS).

2. Revision apps

The revision application is in large business this century, and it appears technology has accomplished what we never thought possible: making revision fun.

In case you're a flash card fanatic you may like to consider StudyBlue, a student application which utilizes your course information to make a choice of card sets for related modification or revision. You can likewise make their own flashcards and test yourself.

Another valuable revision application is GoConqr, which offers resources to make revision charts, mind maps, flash cards, notes and quizzes, just as the ability to connect and collaborate with friends, schoolmates or students from around the world. Another choice is an Exam Countdown (iOS), which monitors the days until every one of your exams.

3. Exam prep apps

There are also student applications now available for exams like the MCAT, GMAT, LSAT and GRE tests, which are utilized by many graduate schools as a part of the admission process. BenchPrep utilizes social networking style functionality which permits users to associate with other test-takers and examine revision materials with quizzes, notes and that's only the tip of the iceberg. You can likewise utilize the application to follow your learning progress.

TCY Exam Prep (Android) is another exam prep application, focused on business students in India and the US. It highlights study resources for MBA/CAT tests, GRE, GATE and bank exams.

4. Student planner apps

Organization is key for success and wellbeing at college universities, and student planner applications are getting progressively popular. Just as saving money on paper, numerous student planner applications likewise send reminders and alerts directly to your phone or device.

Popular student planner applications incorporate Timetable (Android), an application highlighting a sharp and clean interface which you can plan your schedule onto easily. For more familiar looking planner applications for students, consider those with a more conventional spreadsheet- style layout, like My Class Schedule (Android) or Class Timetable (iOS).

5. Bibliography helper apps

If you've ever at any point gone through hours writing up a hefty bibliography, you'll realize exactly how much work goes into ensuring you've remembered all the information for the correct spots in the correct arrangement. EasyBib is an amazingly useful application for students, which makes an academic reference for any book basically from a scan of the book's barcode. As bibliography styles vary depending upon where you study, EasyBib offers referencing in MLA, APA and Chicago styles. Just snap a photo of the barcode or type the title of the book into your device, et voila, proficient referencing done!

6. Video call apps

Most students will know about video calling software like Skype and FaceTime at this point, and this technology is turning out to be considerably more simpler to access as computer companies incorporate quality cameras into their products and internet connection speeds get faster. As the overall technologies improves, so too do the versions available on smaller devices. Utilizing FaceTime on an iPhone has become as simple as making a call, while Skyping on a tablet makes it splendidly possible to prepare dinner/browse the web/compose a text while talking with your parents or friends back home. Other video call applications incorporate imo and Tango.

7. Student safety apps

Various applications for students have been developed promoting personal safety, for use both on-and off-campus. These student applications help to protect users if out alone around evening. The Circle of Six application works on both Android and iOS, and was designed and intended for university students to keep connected with close friends. The application is especially useful for locating friends on nights out and furthermore permits users to send their circle of six friends an instant call for help at the touch of a button. The GPS tracker will mark your location on your friends devices, guaranteeing you'll always be able to find each other if necessary. Other students applications to promote personal safety incorporate bSafe and React Mobile.

8. Wake-up apps

In the event that you end up being a persistent snoozer who consistently awakens around 20 minutes too late for lectures, then Alarmy: Sleep If You Can is the application for you. Alarmy is a alarm application for students which requires users to finish small tasks (like taking a photo of something specific or shaking the telephone all over a number of times) before the alarm turns itself off. Not only will this mean you're ready and raring to go, Alarmy will likewise give you the most recent weather update so you can deside whether to arm yourself with an umbrella before leaving home.

Meanwhile Sleep Cycle aims to correct its users' sleeping pattern by awakening them during their lightest sleep phase. The application does this by observing both movement and the time the user sleep. This implies you should be awaken feeling less groggy in the mornings, and you may likewise get an additional 10 minutes of breakfast time.

9. Responsible-drinking apps

You might be an student, but that doesn't have to mean turning out to be irresponsible when it comes to alcohol. The responsible drinking application WiseDrinking charts how much you've been drinking, gives you suggestions about how much is safe, tells you the ideal opportunity to call a taxi, and maps your location in relation to public transport services.

Using inputs of gender, weight and height, the application computes blood alcohol content (BAC) levels by calculating the amount, type and timing of alcohol consumed and when the user's last meal was. Although the application is totally reliant upon user input and therefore should only be used as a guideline, WiseDrinking can help you remaining aware of your consumption and give you guidance should you feel somewhat worn out.

10. Fun fitness apps

For the individuals who might typically find running boring, the application Zombies, Run! is a fun (and funny) approach to motivate yourself when jogging. The application plays your own music playlists alongside by recorded audio depicting a zombie apocalypse, in which you should run from the infected. Quite frightening, indeed, however as a super-intense workout it's difficult to beat!

This implies you can monitor your nutrition, exercise, hydration and sleep, without checking the progress reports from each application.
